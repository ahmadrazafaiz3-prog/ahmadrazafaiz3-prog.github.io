# Why I Started a GitHub Portfolio Website as a Computer Engineering Student

As a Computer Engineering student, I always thought that university learning was limited to lectures, assignments, quizzes, laboratory work, and examinations. Throughout my academic journey, I collected notes, solved programming problems, prepared for quizzes, and completed laboratory tasks. However, after every semester, most of those learning experiences remained stored in notebooks and computer folders where they were rarely revisited. This changed when I was introduced to the idea of creating a GitHub portfolio website.

The motivation behind creating this website came from Dr. Bilal Ahmad, who taught us Programming Fundamentals and Database Systems. During the semester, he encouraged students to think beyond grades and assignments. One of his suggestions was to create a digital footprint that could represent our learning journey as engineering students. Initially, I considered this to be an optional activity, but as I learned more about professional development and online presence, I realized how valuable this advice was.

A GitHub portfolio website is much more than a collection of files. It serves as a personal learning archive where students can document their experiences, projects, challenges, and lessons learned. Instead of allowing valuable knowledge to disappear after examinations, a blog allows that knowledge to remain accessible for future reference. I decided to use this website as a platform to document my journey through different courses and learning experiences during my Computer Engineering degree.

One reason I found this idea useful is that learning becomes more meaningful when it is documented. During every semester, students spend countless hours studying concepts, solving problems, and preparing for assessments. Unfortunately, much of that knowledge is forgotten after the semester ends. Writing blog posts about those experiences allows me to revisit important concepts and reflect on what I learned. It transforms temporary learning into a permanent record of growth.

During Programming Fundamentals, I learned the importance of problem solving and logical thinking. Many programming concepts initially seemed difficult, but regular practice gradually improved my understanding. Looking back at those experiences and writing about them helps me appreciate the progress I made. It also provides useful information for students who may face similar challenges in the future.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad often discussed the importance of applying knowledge to real-world problems. Beyond teaching Programming Fundamentals and Database Systems, he is actively involved in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning research. One interesting point that he frequently emphasized was the use of medical datasets in machine learning projects. Medical data is generally collected with high precision and accuracy, making it valuable for building reliable AI models. Listening to such discussions helped me understand that engineering education is not only about academic performance but also about creating solutions that can benefit society.

Another advantage of maintaining a GitHub portfolio website is professional visibility. Today, many employers and organizations review online portfolios before conducting interviews. A portfolio website demonstrates not only technical skills but also communication skills, consistency, and dedication to learning. Instead of simply listing courses on a resume, students can provide detailed examples of what they learned and how they applied that knowledge.

This website also helps me connect different learning experiences across semesters. Programming Fundamentals introduced me to logical thinking and software development concepts. Later, Database Systems helped me understand how information is organized, stored, and managed. Future courses will continue building upon these foundations. By documenting these experiences, I can create a continuous story of academic growth rather than isolated records of individual courses.

One lesson I have learned from maintaining this blog is that writing improves understanding. When studying a topic, it is easy to believe that the concept is fully understood. However, explaining the same concept in writing often reveals areas that require additional study. This process encourages deeper learning and helps strengthen technical knowledge.

The website also serves as a source of motivation. Looking back at previous posts reminds me of challenges that seemed difficult at the time but were eventually overcome through effort and persistence. These reflections provide confidence when facing new academic challenges. They also highlight the importance of continuous learning, which is essential in engineering and technology fields.

As I continue my academic journey, I plan to use this website to document experiences from future courses, laboratory work, projects, and independent learning activities. My goal is not to showcase achievements but rather to record genuine learning experiences and lessons learned along the way. I believe that documenting this journey will help me grow as a student while also creating a valuable resource for future reference.

Creating a GitHub portfolio website has already proven to be a worthwhile experience. It allows me to preserve knowledge, improve communication skills, organize learning materials, and maintain a professional digital presence. Most importantly, it helps transform everyday learning experiences into a meaningful record of personal and academic development. Looking ahead, I hope this website continues to grow alongside my journey as a Computer Engineering student.
